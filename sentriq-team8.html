The Product

A Candidate Hiring Captcha. The pre-submission layer upstream of everything.

Cloudflare sits in front of every web request. Platform-agnostic. Upstream of every application. No company built their own Cloudflare. Sentriq is the pre-submission layer for hiring: one integration, every candidate, every ATS, upstream of the entire pipeline. A single trust signal surfaced to humans before the application reaches the recruiter.

Layer 1: Passive
Behavioral fingerprint

Employer-embedded script captures keystroke cadence, paste patterns, device signatures, and session behavior before the candidate submits. Invisible to the applicant. Zero friction.

Layer 2: Active
Async challenge

Sentriq-hosted redirect presents a proprietary behavioral challenge before the application submits. Detects AI completion tools, proxy operators, and mass-apply automation. Candidate consent collected here.

Layer 3: Human
CISO escalation

Anomaly flags route to Sentriq's analyst layer. CISO-confirmed fraud cases enter the cross-company graph. The detection corpus compounds with every employer in the network.

What the employer receives

A trust signal attached to each application before it reaches the ATS. Not a block. Not a rejection. A signal for humans to decide. The recruiter's judgment is preserved. The noise is removed.

The network effect

A fraudster fingerprinted at one employer is flagged at the next. At 15 nodes the graph returns signal no individual company can see. At 100 nodes, coordinated state-backed campaigns are detected before they reach any single company.

Why No Incumbent Can Build This

Every adjacent player is structurally excluded. The architecture requires neutral ground.

The cross-company knowledge graph only works if a single neutral party holds the data. Not the employer. Not the ATS. Not a background check vendor. The candidate consent architecture, the structural neutrality, and the cross-employer data model are unavailable to every incumbent by commercial agreement and product architecture.

ATS Vendors

Customer contracts prohibit cross-employer data sharing. Greenhouse, Ashby, and Workday serve the employer. They cannot build a neutral candidate-consent layer without destroying their core business model.

Background Check Vendors

Checkr, HireRight, and Sterling operate after the damage. After 6 interviews. After $1,900 in process cost. Moving upstream requires a consent model and a behavioral layer neither was built to collect.

Identity Platforms

Persona, Socure, and Onfido confirm the face, not the intent. Identity verification answers "is this person real." It does not answer "is this person who they claim to be applying for this role."
