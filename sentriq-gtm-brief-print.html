GTM Research: Where We Sell and Why

Research conclusions for co-founder alignment. Not for external distribution.

1. Why this research existed

The default instinct when building a hiring product is to target the companies with the most employees. Biggest headcount, biggest problem. That logic is wrong for Sentriq.

We scored the top 100 US companies not by size but by one question: if a fraudulent application gets through, what actually happens? The answer determines whether we belong in a security budget or an HR budget. Security budgets are real. HR budgets are not. That one filter changed the entire list.

2. How we scored every company

Each company scored on five dimensions: Scale, Volume, Pipeline (third-party/offshore suppliers), Exposure (sensitive data or government contracts), and OFH (out-of-country hiring with remote access to US systems).

The key correction: we never count total headcount as the addressable pool. Walmart has 2.1M employees but only ~8K touch payment or pharmacy data at a level where a bad hire creates real liability. Our TAM at Walmart is 8K, not 2.1M. Same logic applies to every company.

Three tests every top-tier target must pass:

  • Test 1: Does a fraudulent hire create regulatory action, national security risk, or financial fraud? Not just an awkward offboarding.
  • Test 2: Does the company hire through offshore pipelines at scale, with those workers accessing US-sensitive systems remotely?
  • Test 3: Is there a CISO with real budget who owns this threat? Not HR.

3. Why big tech is not our bread and butter

Nvidia, Salesforce, Apple, Meta. All large. All hiring at scale. None pass all three tests.

The pattern: big tech has headcount but the consequence of a bad hire is either manageable or lands with a buyer who has no budget. Our sweet spot is where the consequence is irreversible and the buyer is security, not HR.

4. The four tiers that passed the test

Tier 1 — Defense Contractors

Security clearance fraud is a federal crime

One fraudulent cleared hire is not a $50K loss. It is a classified program compromise or an ITAR violation. The pitch is existential. Budget is immediate. Fastest buyer in the market.

Booz Allen Hamilton, Leidos, CACI International, Northrop Grumman, Lockheed Martin, General Dynamics, Boeing, RTX/Raytheon, L3Harris, SAIC, ManTech, BAE Systems US. ~9,000 high-risk hires/year.

Tier 2 — Financial Services

Offshore employees with live access to core banking systems

JPMorgan: 50K+ in India. American Express: 81K in India (largest offshore headcount of any financial firm). Goldman Sachs: Bengaluru is their second-largest office globally. DOJ indicted 14 North Koreans in December 2024 running an $88M operation targeting this sector with deepfakes and stolen identities.

JPMorgan Chase, American Express, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Wells Fargo, Citigroup, Visa, Mastercard, Bank of America, Fidelity. ~40,000-60,000 high-risk hires/year.

Tier 3 — Healthcare / PHI

HIPAA liability follows the data, not the geography of the hire

UnitedHealth has 33,000+ in India with live access to PHI for 190M Americans. Post-Change Healthcare breach (Feb 2024, 190M records exposed), boards are sensitized and CISO budgets are unlocked. Fastest design partner conversation in this tier.

UnitedHealth/Optum, CVS/Aetna, Elevance Health, Humana, Kaiser Permanente, HCA Healthcare, Centene. ~46,000-67,000 high-risk hires/year.

Tier 4 — IT Services / BPO (The Multiplier)

These companies are the supply chain

A DPRK IT worker does not apply to JPMorgan directly. They apply to Cognizant, get placed at JPMorgan, and access the same systems. One deal with Cognizant covers hundreds of their enterprise clients simultaneously. Cognizant: 76% offshore (256,900 in India). TCS: 420,000+ offshore. This is where the network effect compounds.

Cognizant, Infosys, TCS, Accenture, Capgemini, Wipro, Genpact, Concentrix, Teleperformance. ~66,000-139,000 high-risk hires/year.

6. The path to a billion-dollar company

Each tier is a distinct phase, not a simultaneous bet. The sequencing matters.

1

Defense gets us credibility

Booz Allen, Leidos, CACI. Fastest buyers, highest-consequence framing. One signed defense contract is worth more in investor conversations than ten pilot calls in tech.

2

Financial services gets us revenue

JPMorgan, AmEx, Goldman. Highest ACV potential. DPRK threat is explicitly documented against this sector. One enterprise logo closes the Series A.

3

IT services / BPO gets us scale

One deal with Cognizant or TCS covers hundreds of their downstream clients. This is the distribution flywheel that no pure-play hiring product can replicate.

4

Staffing agencies get us the network effect

Per-placement licensing. "Verified by Randstad" becomes a differentiator they sell upstream to clients. Cross-company signal graph starts compounding.

5

Federal contracts get us the moat

Post-FedRAMP, DoD direct contracts, ATS native integrations (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors). No competitor replicates this in under 5 years. $200M+ ARR ceiling.

The one-line conclusion: We are not an HR product that security buys. We are a security product that HR discovers. That distinction determines our pricing, our buyer, our pitch, and our path to a billion. Big tech routes us to HR. Our targets route us to the CISO. That is the entire GTM in one sentence.
