The hiring pipeline is a security problem. Here is the evidence.

The case in three facts

The hiring pipeline is an active attack surface, systematically exploited by nation-states, organized criminal networks, and opportunistic insiders. Every other enterprise perimeter has a security owner. The hiring funnel does not.

DOJ: A US citizen was sentenced to 102 months in federal prison for placing North Korean workers inside 300+ companies. Laptop farms. 68 stolen identities. Named Fortune 500 victims. This is criminal prosecution, not threat research.

Amazon: The Chief Security Officer personally built and runs a program that has blocked 1,800+ DPRK job applicants since April 2024. Not HR. The CSO.

Gartner: "Candidate fraud creates cybersecurity risks that can be far more serious than making a bad hire." 1 in 4 candidate profiles will be fake by 2028.

Top 10 Objection Killers
10 reasons the evidence is unambiguous
1
The DOJ sent someone to federal prison for it.
Christina Chapman: 102 months in federal prison for placing North Korean workers inside 300+ US companies including Fortune 500 firms. $17M generated for the North Korean regime.
DOJ, July 2025
2
Amazon's CSO, not Head of HR, personally runs their defense.
Stephen Schmidt, Amazon Chief Security Officer, built and runs an AI system that blocked 1,800+ North Korean job applications since April 2024. One still slipped through.
Fortune / NBC News, December 2025
3
Gartner's HR research director called it a cybersecurity problem.
"Candidate fraud creates cybersecurity risks that can be far more serious than making a bad hire." Gartner also predicts 25% of all candidate profiles will be fake by 2028.
Jamie Kohn, Gartner Senior Research Director, 2025
4
CrowdStrike ran 304 incident responses from this single attack vector in one year.
FAMOUS CHOLLIMA generated 304 incident response engagements in 2024, 40% classified as insider threat operations. 220% year-over-year growth.
CrowdStrike 2024 Threat Hunting Report
5
FBI presented this at RSA Conference, not at SHRM.
FBI chose RSA Conference 2025 for a dedicated session on DPRK hiring infiltration. FBI Assistant Director Bryan Vorndran. 200 victim notifications issued.
FBI at RSAC 2025
6
KnowBe4 (a security company with mature controls) was breached through their ATS.
KnowBe4 hired a North Korean national who passed four video interviews, background check, and references. He loaded malware on his laptop on day one. Every existing control failed.
KnowBe4 Blog, July 2024
7
Every major threat intelligence vendor has a named threat actor for it.
Mandiant (UNC5267), CrowdStrike (FAMOUS CHOLLIMA), Microsoft (Jasper Sleet), SentinelOne, Okta, and Unit 42 all maintain active named threat actor profiles for hiring-based infiltration.
Multiple vendors, 2024-2025
8
New York's financial regulator told banks to treat job applications as a security threat.
NYDFS formally required every regulated bank, insurer, and fintech to brief their CISO and hiring managers together, and to treat applicant vetting as a cybersecurity control.
NYDFS, November 1, 2024
9
The resume itself is a malware delivery vector.
The More_eggs backdoor targets HR teams with malware-laced resumes delivered via purpose-built candidate sites that bypass security sandboxes. Your email gateway does not block resumes.
eSentire, The Hacker News, SC Media, 2024
10
The EU is making it a compliance obligation by August 2026.
EU AI Act classifies any AI used in recruitment as high-risk. Article 9 mandates documented risk management. Penalties: up to 15M euros or 3% of global revenue. Enforcement begins August 2, 2026.
EU AI Act, Regulation 2024/1689
