The Threat in Detail

The North Korea IT worker operation is not a niche concern. It is a documented, scaled, and active campaign targeting the exact companies that Sentriq is built to protect. The key facts:

The operation
The DPRK Munitions Industry Department runs a program placing IT workers inside Western companies. Estimated 100,000+ workers active globally. Revenue funds weapons programs. Targets: AI research, semiconductor design, GPU architecture.
The method
Stolen US Social Security numbers. Fabricated LinkedIn and GitHub histories. AI-generated profile photos. Live AI tools that feed answers during video interviews. Standard background checks verify the identity - not the person at the keyboard.
KnowBe4, July 2024
A 1,000-person US security company hired a DPRK agent. He passed four rounds of video interviews and a full background check. On day one, his MacBook began installing infostealer malware. He was caught because the laptop was monitored - not because the hiring process worked.
The regulatory response
FBI Cyber Advisory, May 2024. DOJ indictments, October 2024. Senate Armed Services Committee hearings, March 2025. The US government has officially classified DPRK IT worker infiltration as a national security threat.
Why NVIDIA specifically
NVIDIA's GPU and AI architecture is explicitly named in DPRK threat intelligence as a primary target. The combination of export-controlled technology, remote-first engineering hiring, and high-value IP makes NVIDIA's pipeline one of the highest-risk in the world. This is not a generic risk - it is a specific, documented targeting pattern.
The broader problem
DPRK is the extreme case. The underlying method - using AI tools to fabricate a credible candidate identity - is now mainstream. LazyApply sends 1,500 automated applications per day. AI tools generate polished CVs and coach candidates through async video screens in real time. The signal-to-noise ratio in hiring pipelines is collapsing industry-wide. DPRK exploits the exact same vulnerability at a higher stakes level.

The shift-left principle
In security, catching problems earlier reduces cost 100x. Sentriq applies shift-left to hiring: detect fraud at the source, not after the damage.
1
Application Integrity - passive, always on
When a candidate fills out an application, Sentriq captures behavioral metadata in the background: keystroke timing and rhythm, copy-paste events (text typed vs. pasted), time spent per field, field navigation order, session duration patterns. No candidate friction. No candidate awareness. The signals alone are sufficient to flag a large share of synthetic applications.
2
Async Challenge - triggered on anomaly
When Layer 1 flags an anomaly above threshold, the candidate is invited to complete a 2-3 minute task - a short written or practical response relevant to the role, calibrated to their claimed profile. Sentriq evaluates behavioral signals and content quality during the response: typing pattern, hesitation, correction behavior, response timing, and demonstrated knowledge. A person being coached or impersonated cannot replicate both the behavioral fingerprint and the knowledge depth of the individual whose CV was submitted. This is the core detection mechanism for DPRK-style proxy fraud.
3
Human Review - analyst, not recruiter
Cases flagged by Layer 2 are reviewed by a Sentriq-trained analyst before anything reaches the employer's recruiting team. The analyst reviews the behavioral signal, the challenge response, and any corroborating indicators. The employer's hiring team always makes the final decision - Sentriq never rejects a candidate unilaterally. This layer is also the EU AI Act Annex III compliance answer: high-risk AI hiring systems must have human oversight in the decision loop.
Why behavioral signals and not identity verification
Identity verification confirms that a credential exists - not that the person in front of you matches the credential. DPRK agents use real stolen Social Security numbers. They pass identity checks. Behavioral signals detect whether the person applying now behaves like the person who built that profile. That is a fundamentally different and complementary signal. Sentriq does not replace background checks - it detects what background checks cannot.

Section 3

The Data Moat

The three detection layers are the product. The cross-company anomaly graph is the structural advantage that compounds over time and cannot be replicated by any incumbent.

Why ATS vendors cannot build this
Every major ATS vendor - Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, SAP SuccessFactors - holds ATS contracts that explicitly prohibit cross-employer data sharing. This is a legal constraint, not a technical one. They cannot build a cross-company fraud signal without violating their own customer contracts. This constraint is permanent. It does not change if they acquire better detection technology.

How the graph compounds

Customer 1
Detection based on absolute behavioral anomalies. Deviations from normal human typing and session behavior are flagged. No prior data required. Useful from day one.
Customer 10
Cross-company patterns emerge. The same behavioral fingerprint or synthetic identity that applied to three other companies in the past 30 days is flagged the moment they apply to a fourth - without any company sharing data with another.
Customer 100
Coordinated campaigns detected at the cluster level. Multiple fake identities originating from the same behavioral cluster - the signature of a DPRK-style operation - are identified as a campaign, not just individual suspicious applications.

The consent architecture that makes it legal

Sentriq is a neutral third party. Candidates interact directly with Sentriq's own interface under Sentriq's own privacy notice before any behavioral data is collected. The employer does not collect the behavioral data - Sentriq does. This means the cross-company graph is built on direct candidate consent to a neutral platform, not on employer-to-employer data sharing. This architecture is permanently unavailable to any player that sits inside the hiring stack.
