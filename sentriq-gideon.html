The Problem

You cannot tell who is real
anymore. Nobody can.

AI tools have made it trivial to generate a perfect CV, pass keyword screening, and deliver polished async video answers. Every recruiter knows the signal has degraded. The pipeline is fuller than ever and harder than ever to read. That is the everyday problem. Then there is the extreme version of it.

The everyday version
AI-generated applications flood pipelines at a volume no team can manually review. Screening tools are gamed by the same methods that create them. Recruiters spend most of their time on candidates they cannot verify, and the ones they miss are not random.
The extreme version
North Korea runs a documented operation placing workers inside Western technology companies using the exact same techniques. KnowBe4 hired one in 2024. He passed four video interviews and a full background check. He was caught on day one when his laptop started installing malware.
The method is the same. The stakes differ. Sentriq addresses both because the fix is identical.
What We Built

We built the attack first.
Now we build the defense.

Last year we built Huntwise — a tool that exploits applicant tracking systems the same way adversaries do. 73,500 lines of code. 30 live experiments across three ATS platforms. 17 documented exploitation techniques the ATS vendors themselves did not know existed. That research is the foundation of Sentriq. We know exactly how the fraud works because we built it.

1
Application Integrity
Behavioral signals captured at submission. Keystroke dynamics, copy-paste detection, session timing, field navigation patterns. Passive. No candidate friction.
2
Async Challenge
When anomalies are detected, the candidate completes a short task. We score behavioral biometrics during the response, not just the content. A proxy cannot replicate the behavioral baseline of the person they are impersonating.
3
Human Review
Flagged cases go to a trained analyst before anything reaches a recruiter. The hiring team always makes the final call. This is also the EU AI Act answer — Annex III classifies hiring AI as high-risk; human review in the loop is the legal requirement.
