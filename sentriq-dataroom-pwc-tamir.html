Section 01

Business Context

The minimum context needed to evaluate the financial model. Not a pitch. Just the facts required to understand the numbers.

What it isA pre-submission trust layer for job applications. Detects fraudulent applications (DPRK-type operatives, proxy candidates, AI-assisted fraud) before they enter an ATS. Outputs a trust signal per application. Human-in-the-loop. No automated rejections.
Product stageDemo complete. Engineering underway. No live product in production. No paying customers. Design partner conversations active.
CategoryNew. Called "Candidate Hiring Captcha" internally. Closest analogy: Cloudflare as a pre-ATS layer. Platform-agnostic, upstream of everything.
BuyersDual buyer: CISO (security framing) and HR / TA leader (quality framing). Enterprise contracts require both. This is the primary sales complexity.
Revenue modelSaaS subscription. Two-tier enterprise pricing. Tier 1 Enterprise Anchor: $240K ACV. Tier 2 BPO/Growth: $84K ACV. Blended: $150K ACV. Annual contracts. Churn target 10%. NDR target 115%.
Revenue gateSOC 2 Type I. Enterprise procurement will not open without it. Modeled at Q3 2026 post-seed. This is the primary risk in the model.
FoundersSamuel Kemper (CEO, GTM, IDF intelligence background) and Ben Czarninski (CTO, engineering). Israel-based. Both IDF intelligence unit veterans.
Regulatory driverEU AI Act Article 6 Annex III point 4: AI tools in hiring are high-risk systems. Compliance deadline August 2, 2026. Every enterprise using AI-assisted screening is in scope. This accelerates the buyer timeline.
On the enterprise seed model

The standard Israeli cybersecurity capital sequence: research, demo, design partners, seed close, SOC 2, first enterprise customer, Series A. Sentriq is at step 3. The model is seed-to-enterprise-readiness, not seed-to-product-market-fit. The distinction matters when evaluating the traction timeline.

Headcount and loaded costs

Series A benchmarks

Are the Israeli cyber Series A metrics we are targeting consistent with what you are seeing in the market today? Has the bar shifted post-2022?

On financial services clients

HSBC and Wells Fargo procurement

We have warm paths to HSBC and Wells Fargo. Maya's direct knowledge is the reason she is in this meeting. How long does a new security vendor typically take to move from first meeting to a paid pilot at a Tier 1 bank? Is SOC 2 Type I sufficient, or do they require Type II? What is the procurement checklist from the inside?

Deal size at FS scale

We model Tier 1 FS enterprise at $240K ACV. At a bank with 80,000+ employees (HSBC, Wells Fargo), is that the right order of magnitude, or are deals typically structured per-region, per-entity, or volume-tiered? Is a phased pilot-to-annual structure standard for a new security layer in this segment?

EU AI Act as a procurement accelerant

We are modeling the EU AI Act August 2026 deadline as a compliance driver that accelerates procurement decisions for European and global financial institutions. Is this realistic from PwC's current advisory work with FS clients, or is it too early for procurement teams to be acting on it?
