You Have Been Here Before

Each of these incidents shares the same root: unverified actors entered J.P. Morgan systems through a trusted channel. No perimeter tool caught them because none sat at the entry point.

Charlie Javice / Frank Acquisition (2021-2025)
$175M acquisition. 4.25 million synthetic accounts.
Frank founder Charlie Javice paid a data scientist $18,000 to generate 4.25 million fake user records. Those records passed J.P. Morgan's third-party due diligence because the verifier counted data fields instead of authenticating users. JPMorgan paid $175M for a company with fewer than 300,000 real customers. Javice was sentenced to 85 months in federal prison in September 2025.
Exact attack vector: synthetic data bypasses process-level verification. The same attack runs against your ATS today.
Source: DOJ, CBS News, CNN (2025). $287.5M total restitution ordered.
2014 JPMorgan Data Breach
83 million accounts. One compromised credential.
Attackers entered through a single unpatched server tied to a corporate charity race website. One employee credential, compromised via malware, gave two months of undetected access. 76 million households and 7 million small businesses exposed. JPMorgan doubled its cybersecurity budget to $500M in response.
The pattern: a single trusted entry point, insufficient verification, months of invisible access.
Source: Washington Post, BitSight analysis, 2014-2015.
Sons and Daughters Hiring Program (2016)
$264M settlement. Hiring used as an access mechanism.
J.P. Morgan maintained a formal, spreadsheet-tracked program hiring unqualified relatives of foreign government officials in exchange for business. 100 unqualified hires. The program generated over $100M in revenues and triggered joint enforcement by the SEC, DOJ, and Federal Reserve.
Proof point: who you hire is a regulated activity. The hiring pipeline is a documented risk vector, not a back-office function.
Source: SEC Press Release, November 2016. FCPA violation.

How Sentriq Works

Think of Sentriq as Cloudflare for the hiring pipeline. Cloudflare sits between the internet and your web infrastructure, invisible to legitimate users, lethal to automated attacks. Sentriq sits between candidates and your ATS. Real applicants pass through without friction. Manufactured applications do not.

1

Behavioral Baseline

Every application carries a behavioral fingerprint: timing patterns, session depth, form interaction, device signals. A human filling out a job application behaves differently from a script running at scale. Sentriq reads that signal before the application reaches your ATS.

2

Trust Layer

Cross-referenced against known threat patterns and cross-company signals. The same submission behavior flagged at another institution surfaces as a trust signal here. A fraudulent actor cannot reset their behavioral fingerprint across companies.

3

Async Personalized Challenge

For applications that cross a risk threshold, Sentriq serves a personalized async challenge that a script cannot complete. Not a generic CAPTCHA. A task calibrated to the role and the signal. Humans pass. Automated submissions drop out.

Sentriq is platform-agnostic. It operates as a pre-submission layer, upstream of any ATS. No rip-and-replace. No change to recruiter workflow. Trust signals surface inside your existing recruiter interface.
