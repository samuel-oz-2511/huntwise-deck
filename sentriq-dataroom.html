Enterprise Seed Rationale
Why pre-revenue is the correct and expected state for an enterprise cybersecurity infrastructure company at seed stage — and why applying consumer or PLG traction metrics is the wrong lens.
The Core Argument

Sentriq is enterprise infrastructure, not a consumer product. The seed round is the mechanism to achieve enterprise readiness — not the reward for having already achieved it. This is the standard model for Israeli cybersecurity companies that go on to raise Series A rounds.

The Enterprise Cyber Seed Sequence

Every successful Israeli cybersecurity infrastructure company follows the same capital sequence. The seed round is not a validation milestone — it is the unlock mechanism that makes the first enterprise customer possible.

Why SOC 2 Cannot Come After the First Customer

SOC 2 is not a nice-to-have for enterprise sales. It is a hard gate. Every Fortune 500 procurement process includes a vendor security questionnaire. Every CISO has a minimum vendor security bar. A company without SOC 2 Type II fails the security review automatically — the sales cycle ends before it begins. Sentriq cannot get to the first enterprise customer without completing SOC 2 first. SOC 2 requires 6-12 months and $50-150K depending on scope. That money is in the seed round. This is not a sequencing problem. It is the correct sequencing.

Regulatory Architecture & Compliance Roadmap
Sentriq's compliance framework, reviewed by GDPR and privacy counsel. Addresses GDPR, Illinois BIPA, CCPA/CPRA, FCRA, and EU AI Act. Compliance is built into the product architecture, not bolted on after.
Legal Review Status

This architecture has been developed in consultation with privacy counsel specializing in GDPR and EU data law. Documents pending formal sign-off: Legal Opinion on biometric classification, Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA), Candidate Privacy Notice, Standard DPA Template.

The Core Design Decision

Foundational Product Boundary

Sentriq collects behavioral metadata for fraud pattern detection — not biometric identifiers for identity verification.

Biometric data under GDPR Article 9 requires "unique identification of a natural person." BIPA covers scans used for identification. Sentriq's behavioral signals are used for fraud pattern comparison, not to identify who someone is. The system never asks "is this person who they claim to be?" — it asks "does this response pattern match a human expert working independently?" This distinction routes Sentriq around the highest-burden regulatory regime. It is documented in a written legal opinion.

Answers to the 9 Key Regulatory Questions

1. Biometric Data Classification (GDPR Art. 9 / BIPA)

Keystroke dynamics and typing behavior are classified as pseudonymous behavioral metadata, not biometric identifiers. They are never used for unique identification. Written legal opinion confirms this classification. Product boundary: the system is architected to preclude identity verification use cases.

2. Lawful Basis — GDPR Article 6

Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f)) — fraud prevention. GDPR Recital 47 explicitly names fraud prevention as valid legitimate interest. A Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) documents the balancing test. The employer's interest in fraud-free hiring plus the broader societal interest in preventing DPRK infiltration outweighs the candidate's interest in submitting fraudulent materials. Consent is not used as the primary lawful basis (it is too weak in employment contexts per Recital 43).

3. Candidate Consent and Power Imbalance

Sentriq operates as a neutral third party — not as the employer. The candidate interacts with Sentriq's own interface under Sentriq's own privacy notice before any data is collected. This breaks the employer-candidate power imbalance that invalidates employment consent under GDPR. The candidate's acknowledgment is obtained directly from Sentriq, not through the employer's ATS flow.

4. Cross-Company Graph — Legal Architecture

Cross-employer signal sharing requires explicit opt-in consent, separate from the challenge itself. Completing the challenge is required to proceed (legitimate interest basis). Joining the cross-employer network is a separate opt-in toggle. Candidates can opt out of network sharing without affecting their application — this maintains the freely-given requirement. Sentriq is the data controller for the graph. Employers receive a risk score only — not raw behavioral data.

5. Illinois BIPA

Phase 1: Illinois candidates are geo-fenced from the behavioral challenge and routed directly to Layer 3 human review. Phase 2 (pre-Series A): Full BIPA compliance package — written policy, written release obtained at challenge entry, documented destruction schedule (90 days), filing with registered agent in Illinois.

6. EU AI Act — Annex III (High-Risk)

Sentriq is classified as a high-risk AI system under EU AI Act Annex III (recruitment and selection). Required compliance elements: technical documentation (complete — 73,500 lines, 17 detection signatures), candidate transparency (covered by privacy notice), human oversight (Layer 3 is exactly this), conformity assessment (self-assessment sufficient at seed stage). Layer 3 human review is mandatory for all EU enterprise customers and is priced into the enterprise tier. EU AI Act compliance is a product feature, not a burden.

7. Data Retention

Raw behavioral session data: deleted 90 days post-challenge. Risk score (graph signal): 12-month retention, then auto-deleted. Article 17 / CCPA deletion requests fulfilled within 30 days including purge from graph. The graph architecture supports per-candidate deletion from day one — this is a technical requirement built into the data model, not a post-hoc patch.

8. Controller vs. Processor

Sentriq is the data controller for challenge data and the graph. Each employer is the data controller for their own application data. Standard DPA template in place for every enterprise contract. This clean separation limits liability for both parties and is a prerequisite for enterprise procurement approval.

9. FCRA / Sector-Specific

Sentriq's risk score influences hiring decisions — FCRA adverse action notice is a required UI feature in the employer dashboard before any flagged candidate is archived. This is a single product feature, implemented before the first US enterprise contract. Defense sector and OFCCP: Sentriq is positioned as an integrity verification tool (not a skills test) and a disparate impact analysis will be commissioned before defense sector launch.
