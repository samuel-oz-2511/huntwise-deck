Security Companies Are Prime Targets

DPRK and state actors specifically target security firms for infiltration. An operative inside a company like Zenity has access to enterprise security tooling, customer environments, and threat intelligence. That is not a headcount error. That is an adversarial intrusion. The hiring funnel is the attack surface nobody is watching.

Zenity's thesis: threats enter trusted systems through legitimate-looking channels - low-code apps, AI agents, SaaS integrations. The same thesis applies to hiring. A fake candidate is a threat that enters through the most trusted channel of all: your own recruitment process.

The Product

The Candidate Hiring Captcha.

The integrity layer between candidates and your hiring team. Three signal layers. Zero auto-rejections. Every flag is a human decision.

🚫
Layer 1: Auto-Flag

Application integrity engine detects synthetic resumes, identity inconsistencies, device fingerprint anomalies, and bot-pattern submissions. Flags route to review. Not removed. Reviewed.

⏱
Layer 2: Async Challenge

A 2-minute task sent to the candidate. Captures keystroke dynamics, time-to-first-response, copy-paste behavior, and revision patterns. No live session. No scheduling friction. Async from the start.

👁
Layer 3: Human Review

Your recruiter sees an evidence card: what was flagged, why, what the behavioral signal shows. They decide. Zero auto-rejections. Every flag is a human call. Fully defensible under EU AI Act.
