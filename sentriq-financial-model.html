Revenue Assumptions

Hybrid pricing model: base retainer by tier plus consumption on application volume above tier floor. Every tier requires ATS integration and SDK installation: there is no self-serve. Growth (50-500 employees, integration-assisted, floor: 500 challenges/month, one-time onboarding fee $2,000): $9,600/yr base retainer ($799/month). Requires a technical contact at the customer to handle the ATS webhook and landing page SDK. Growth tier opens Q4 2026, gated on ATS marketplace listings (Greenhouse, Lever) that reduce integration friction to near-zero. Scale (500-2,500 employees, sales-assisted, floor: 2,000 challenges/month, Layer 3 included): $36,000/yr base retainer ($2,999/month). Mid-Market (global financial services, BPO, enterprise tech, 2,500-10,000 employees): $84,000/yr base retainer. Enterprise Anchor (HSBC, Booking Holdings scale, 10,000+ employees): $240,000/yr base retainer. Consumption above tier floor is billed per application at a per-unit rate set at contract time. Two Enterprise Anchor customers processing 600K and 800K candidates/year pay the same $240K retainer but different consumption totals: the 800K customer pays materially more. This is the primary NDR driver. The stated ACVs are retainer floors, not ceilings. No Starter tier pre-Series A: a sub-50-employee company typically lacks the technical resource to complete ATS integration; this tier becomes viable post-Series A once native ATS marketplace listings handle the integration automatically. Blended retainer floor (Growth + Scale + Mid-Market + Enterprise Anchor, 20/30/30/20 mix): $86,000/yr. Actual ARR per logo will exceed retainer as consumption accrues. Series A metric uses Scale + Mid-Market + Enterprise Anchor logos only. Churn: 15% Year 1 (no retention history, modeled conservatively); 10% from Year 2 as ATS integration creates workflow dependency that reduces switching. NDR target: 115% via consumption expansion as hiring volume grows, plus scope expansion across business units. Design partners: 20% first-year discount on retainer, applied at annual contract execution (not during the pilot period). Pilot pricing is full monthly retainer: Q3 2026 Scale DPs pay $3K/mo at pilot rate; the 20% discount activates on the annual commitment that follows pilot success (Enterprise Anchor: $192K year 1; Mid-Market: $67K year 1; Scale: $29K year 1). Growth tier includes onboarding fee ($2K) as the first revenue event; monthly retainer plus consumption follows. BPOs price as Mid-Market with a channel multiplier: one BPO contract covers multiple enterprise hiring environments.

When to Raise the SAFE

Optimal window: Q2 2027 at $35K MRR, when the first pilot-to-annual conversions are signed and the traction story is priced on proof, not promise. Why the Unit Economics Hold

The LTV:CAC of 15x holds across both buyer paths. LTV is calculated on the actual trigger-point customer mix (30% Growth / 40% Scale / 20% Mid-Market / 10% Enterprise), giving a $58K blended ACV. 3-year gross profit: Year 1 $45,240 + Year 2 $38,454 + Year 3 $34,609 = $118,303. LTV:CAC = $118K / $8K = 15x. This is calculated on the conservative side: the blended ACV at steady state (once more Mid-Market and Enterprise logos are added post-Series A) returns to the $86K blended figure modeled in the pricing section. CAC is blended lower ($8K) because Growth tier customers are self-serve: no AE cost, no multi-month sales cycle. Scale and Enterprise CAC is ~$10K with founder-led sales in Year 1. CAC payback is 2 months blended ($8K / ($58K × 78% / 12)); at Mid-Market tier ($84K ACV) it drops to 1.5 months; at Enterprise Anchor ($240K ACV) it falls below 1 month. The 5-month enterprise sales cycle is time to close, not time to recover CAC. On churn: 15% first-year churn is modeled conservatively. The 90%+ gross retention target is Year 2+ steady state, supported by ATS workflow embedding, EU AI Act compliance dependency, and consumption-based auto-expansion. NDR of 115% is structurally supported by hiring volume growth (more candidates = more challenges, no upsell call) and scope expansion across business units. Retainer floors ($9.6K, $36K, $84K, $240K) are minimums: actual revenue per logo runs 15-30% above retainer at steady state for Scale and above. On the $58K ACV vs $82K/mo revenue model: the $58K blended ACV is the retainer floor per logo at the trigger-point customer mix. The $82K/mo in the burn table is the quarterly average MRR for Q4 2027, not the December exit rate: 6 of the 20 logos close during Q4 (not all on Oct 1), so the quarter-average is below the end-of-quarter run rate. December 2027 exit MRR = 20 logos × $58K / 12 = $96.7K/mo = ~$1.16M ARR run rate. The $984K figure ($82K × 12) is the conservative presentation; the actual Series A raise opens from a higher ARR exit rate.
