Runway Interpretation

$3.21M remaining at Series A trigger means the raise happens from a position of strength - not pressure. If the Series A is delayed 6 months, Sentriq can operate without bridging. If an enterprise deal slips, there is buffer. The $5M seed is sized for a realistic enterprise sales environment, not an optimistic one. Israeli cyber seed rounds comparable to this (Apono, Armory, Laminar) all raised $4-6M on similar timelines.

Why the Security & Compliance Line Is Non-Negotiable

SOC 2 Type II is a hard gate for enterprise procurement. No Fortune 500 CISO signs a contract with a vendor that cannot pass security review. The compliance spend is not overhead - it is the sales unlock. Without it, the entire revenue model is blocked. This is the standard Israeli cyber seed dynamic: raise capital, get enterprise-ready, land first customer, raise Series A.

Why the Unit Economics Hold

The LTV:CAC of 16x looks high - here's why it's credible. CAC is low because (1) founder-led sales eliminates AE cost in Year 1 and (2) the DPRK/deepfake news cycle drives inbound - the FBI advisory and KnowBe4 incident are the best top-of-funnel a security company can have. Churn is low because (1) the product sits in the hiring workflow - a workflow that doesn't change quarter to quarter - and (2) the EU AI Act creates regulatory lock-in for every EU-facing customer. NDR of 115% is conservative: as a company hires more people, it runs more challenges, increasing the per-challenge revenue naturally without any upsell motion.

The Conservative Case Still Works

This model assumes no inbound press-driven deals, no channel partnerships, and a 3-month average sales cycle with no fast closes. Even in the conservative case - half the forecast customer count - Sentriq reaches $500K ARR with $3M+ cash remaining, giving it the runway to bridge to Series A without a down round. The $5M seed is not sized to the optimistic case. It is sized to the realistic one.
