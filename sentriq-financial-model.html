Revenue Assumptions

Three-tier enterprise model. Tier 1 Enterprise Anchor (HSBC, Wells Fargo, Standard Chartered scale, 10,000+ employees): $240,000 ACV. Tier 2 Mid-Market (Ubiquity scale, 2,500-10,000 employees): $84,000 ACV. Tier 3 Design Partner Entry (WalkMe scale, 1,000-2,500 employees): $36,000 ACV. Commercial blended (Tier 1 + Tier 2): $150,000 ACV (40% Tier 1, 60% Tier 2). Tier 3 is the proof-of-concept tier -- design partners at this scale validate the product and generate unconditional reference rights. The commercial GTM and Series A revenue model targets Tier 1 and Tier 2 exclusively. Churn: 15% Year 1 (no retention history -- modeled conservatively); 10% from Year 2 as workflow integration and EU AI Act compliance dependency reduce switching. NDR target: 115% via two mechanisms: headcount growth drives additional per-application volume at existing accounts; most logos start with one region or business unit and expand to full deployment without a new sale. Design partners: 20% first-year discount (Tier 1: $192K year 1; Tier 2: $67K year 1; Tier 3: $29K year 1). BPOs price as Tier 2 with a channel multiplier: one BPO contract covers multiple enterprise hiring environments.

Why the Security & Compliance Line Is Non-Negotiable

SOC 2 Type II is a hard gate for enterprise procurement. No Fortune 500 CISO signs a contract with a vendor that cannot pass security review. The compliance spend is not overhead - it is the sales unlock. Without it, the entire revenue model is blocked. This is the standard Israeli cyber seed dynamic: raise capital, get enterprise-ready, land first customer, raise Series A.

Why the Unit Economics Hold

The LTV:CAC of 16x looks high - here's why it's credible. CAC is low because (1) founder-led sales eliminates AE cost in Year 1 and (2) the hiring fraud news cycle drives inbound -- proxy worker incidents, adversarial insertion cases, and FBI advisories on coordinated IT worker fraud are the best top-of-funnel a security company can have. On churn: we model 15% first-year churn (85% retention), not 10%. A pre-revenue product with no renewal history cannot credibly claim 10% churn. The 90%+ gross retention target is Year 2+ steady state, supported by three structural factors: (1) the product embeds directly in the hiring workflow, which enterprises do not swap out quarter to quarter; (2) EU AI Act enforcement from August 2026 creates a compliance dependency for every EU-facing customer; (3) usage expands naturally as hiring volume grows, making downgrade the path of most resistance. Best-in-class infrastructure companies earn 90%+ retention -- we expect to reach it, but we model the honest ramp. NDR of 115% is conservative: as a company hires more people, it runs more challenges, increasing per-challenge revenue naturally. Scope expansion adds a second layer: most enterprise logos start with one region or business unit; full deployment across all hiring pipelines is a standard expansion event, not a new sale. Both drivers compound in enterprise accounts with large, distributed hiring operations.

The Conservative Case Still Works

This model assumes no inbound press-driven deals, no channel partnerships, and a 3-month average sales cycle with no fast closes. Even in the conservative case - half the forecast customer count - Sentriq reaches $500K ARR with $3M+ cash remaining, giving it the runway to bridge to Series A without a down round. The $5M seed is not sized to the optimistic case. It is sized to the realistic one.
