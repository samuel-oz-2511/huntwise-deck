Logo Refinement: The Gate

Sentriq

April 2026
Why red is out. Red carries too many meanings in enterprise security: breach notification, alert state, danger flag. None of those align with how Sentriq positions itself. Sentriq is infrastructure, not an alarm. The buyers are CISOs at defense contractors, financial services firms, and healthcare systems. Their color language is institutional navy, precision slate, and carbon. The gate form is intact and stronger. The signal beacon above it now speaks in a palette these buyers already trust.

Slate + Amber

Direction 03
Carbon + Gold
Enterprise authority without electric energy. The carbon structure reads as weight, permanence, earned presence. The gold signal is not amber, not yellow: it reads as trust through restraint. This is the boardroom palette. When Sentriq is in a room with Northrop Grumman, Goldman Sachs, or UnitedHealth legal teams, this mark says credibility. Gold separates Sentriq from the startup-blue cybersecurity cluster without going warm enough to feel like HR software.

03 — What Changed in the Icon
Before
Sentriq
  • Single color: crimson throughout
  • Bezier arch (cubic curve, uncontrolled geometry)
  • Simple filled rectangles, no capital or base
  • Beacon is a filled circle only, no signal
  • No threshold between the pillars
After
Sentriq
  • Two-color system: structure + signal separated
  • True semicircular arch (precise geometric arc)
  • Pillar caps and bases: architectural weight
  • Beacon radiates: two concentric signal arcs
  • Threshold line at base: the crossing point is visible
