Section 01

What We Are Building

A Candidate Hiring Captcha. The Cloudflare for job applications.

Think about how Cloudflare works. When you fill out a web form and hit submit, Cloudflare runs bot detection before you ever reach the site. If you are a human, nothing happens. If you are a bot, you get a challenge. The site owner gets one signal per suspicious request. No automated blocking. A human makes the final call.
Sentriq is exactly this, applied to job applications. We sit upstream of the ATS (the system recruiters use to receive applications), before the application reaches any human's queue. Every application passes through our behavioral analysis. Anomalous ones get a trust challenge. The recruiter sees one clear signal in their review queue: look at this one. No automated rejections. No biometrics. Human in the loop for every decision.
We call it a Candidate Hiring Captcha. The output is a trust signal per application, not a score. It surfaces in the recruiter's existing workflow. Zero changes to how hiring teams work.

What it is not

Not a background checkBackground checks screen identity after an offer. Sentriq screens application authenticity before the first human review. Different timing, different buyer, different budget line.
Not an ATSWe do not replace Greenhouse, Workday, Lever, or any ATS. We sit in front of them as a pre-submission layer. Platform-agnostic. Upstream of everything.
Not identity verificationWe do not ask "is this person who they claim to be?" We ask "does this application look like it was written by the person who claims to have this background?" Different question, different legal framework, different product.
Not an automated rejection systemSentriq never rejects anyone automatically. It surfaces a signal. A human decides. This is intentional: it is how we stay on the right side of employment law and the EU AI Act.
The unit of analysis

Sentriq analyzes the application, not the candidate. This matters legally and in product framing. The submission event (the act of applying) is what we evaluate, not the person. An application can be fraudulent even from a real person (e.g. a proxy who wrote the resume for them). Keep this in mind for any customer conversation.

Section 04

How the Product Works

Three layers. Each one addresses a different attack vector. You need to know all three well to support enterprise customers.

1
Layer 1: Behavioral Baseline
Runs passively on every application. No friction for candidates. Analyzes application metadata: typing cadence, form completion patterns, paste behavior, timing anomalies, cross-platform submission signals, device and network fingerprinting. Flags statistical outliers automatically. This is the silent layer. Most applications pass through with no signal generated.
CS context: when an employer asks "how does it affect the candidate experience?", the answer is Layer 1 is invisible to real candidates. They never know it is there.
2
Layer 2: Trust Layer (Behavioral Challenge)
Triggered only for applications flagged by Layer 1. Approximately 5-10% of all applications. A short behavioral challenge calibrated to the specific role and claimed background. Not a quiz. Not an assessment. A challenge designed so that a real expert in the claimed field completes it naturally, while any automation tool, proxy writer, or AI-generated response fails. Triggered asynchronously: the candidate gets a link, not an interruption mid-application.
CS context: this is where enterprise customers will have questions. The challenge must feel appropriate to the seniority and type of role. This is a key product design area where your input will matter.
3
Layer 3: Async Personalized Challenge (Human Review)
Escalated applications (typically 1-3% of all applications) go to a Sentriq Fraud Analyst for human review. The analyst interprets the Layer 1 and Layer 2 signals, reviews the full application context, and surfaces a single trust signal into the employer's ATS review queue. This is the EU AI Act-compliant oversight layer. It is also the product layer that makes enterprise CISOs comfortable: a trained human made the call, not an algorithm.
CS context: you will be deeply involved in this layer. The Fraud Analyst function, the quality of the trust signal output, and the employer dashboard experience are all areas where your operational and product background applies directly.

What the employer sees

In their existing ATS review queue, each application has one additional field: a Sentriq trust signal. Green means nothing flagged. A caution flag means review this application before advancing. No scores. No rejection recommendations. No biometric data. One human-readable signal. The recruiter's workflow is unchanged except they can now prioritize the queue by signal.

What the candidate sees

For Layer 1: nothing. For Layer 2: a brief follow-up challenge delivered asynchronously, framed as a final application step. Transparent, respectful, and appropriate to the role. For most candidates, the experience is identical to any application. Only flagged applications get a challenge, and only the most anomalous cases go to human review.
Why this is directly in your wheelhouse

You spent three years building Quack AI, trainable AI agents for customer experience. You know what it takes to design a human-in-the-loop AI workflow, handle escalation paths gracefully, and make the employer-facing interface feel like a natural part of their existing workflow. Layer 3 and the employer dashboard are the exact product problems you have already solved in a different domain. This is not a stretch for you. It is your home turf.

The Moat

Four structural advantages that cannot be bought or copied quickly.

1. The attack research corpus

73,500 lines of empirical data built from running live attacks inside real ATS platforms over 18 months. A competitor starting today needs 18 months minimum before their detection model is meaningful. With Huntwise continuing to run with 100+ beta users, our corpus grows every day.

2. The behavioral challenge design

The challenge is calibrated to the specific claimed role and background. Designing a challenge that a real expert passes naturally but a proxy or AI fails requires deep knowledge of how fraud tools work. This is the hardest part to replicate. It requires knowing exactly what the attacker does.

3. The cross-company graph

Every employer that joins Sentriq contributes to a signal graph. A candidate who fabricated credentials at Company A is flagged when they apply to Company B, at zero additional cost to Company B. This network effect strengthens with every new customer. No ATS can build this. Their customer contracts prohibit cross-employer data sharing. Permanently unavailable to them. Sentriq's neutral-party architecture is the only legal path to this graph.

4. Founder-market fit that cannot be replicated

Samuel ran ATS platforms professionally for 18 months as a recruiter and watched the signal collapse happen from the inside. Ben built the attack tooling that exploits ATS vulnerabilities. No one else in this space has built both sides. The detection signatures are reverse-engineered from live attack techniques by the person who built them.

Why the cross-company graph matters for enterprise CS

When you are in a customer conversation with a large enterprise and they ask "what do we get from other customers' data?", the answer is: every employer on Sentriq makes the signal richer for every other employer. A campaign targeting one company is visible across the network. This is a selling point and a retention driver. Once an enterprise is in the graph, leaving means giving up that signal. That is structural retention, not contractual lock-in.
