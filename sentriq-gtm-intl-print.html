GTM Research: Rest of World

Same scoring framework as Group 1 (US). Key difference: the EU AI Act creates a compliance purchase driver that does not exist in the US market.

Why international is a separate group

The US GTM (Group 1) is a security purchase. The CISO is the primary buyer. The pitch is insider threat, DPRK, offshore pipeline risk. It is a discretionary budget decision driven by threat urgency.

The international GTM (Group 2) has all of those same dynamics — plus one that does not exist in the US: a regulatory mandate with a hard deadline and criminal-level penalties. The EU AI Act changes the buyer conversation from "should we buy this" to "we are legally required to implement this by August 2026."

The EU AI Act — The Compliance Purchase Driver

AI systems used in recruitment are classified as HIGH-RISK under Annex III of the EU AI Act. This covers resume screening, candidate scoring, job ad targeting, video interview analysis, and any system that filters, ranks, or selects applicants.

Hard deadline: August 2, 2026. Every company using AI in hiring within the EU must comply or face penalties of up to €35M or 7% of global annual turnover.

Article 26 requires a qualified human to oversee every AI-assisted hiring decision with the power to intervene and modify the output. That is exactly what Sentriq's human review layer does.

Why this matters for the dual-buyer problem: In the US, CISO and HR are two separate budget conversations. In Europe, the EU AI Act forces Legal and HR Risk into the same compliance purchase that CISO is already making for fraud prevention. The dual-buyer stalemate partially dissolves. Three buyers now align on one product: CISO (fraud), HR Risk (compliance), Legal (liability).

Affected companies: every enterprise using LinkedIn Recruiter, Greenhouse, Workday, or SAP SuccessFactors. Virtually every European enterprise with 500+ employees is already non-compliant.

Three additional factors that do not exist in Group 1

1. DPRK has explicitly migrated to Europe

Google Threat Intelligence Group confirmed April 2025: DPRK IT workers are actively targeting Germany, Portugal, and the UK. Infiltrations are up 220% in 12 months. Any European CISO briefed on this responds immediately.

2. Australia's offshore backlash creates a board-level buyer

Australian banks are being publicly shamed in parliament for offshore hiring. Commonwealth Bank cut 304 Australian IT jobs and immediately posted the same roles in India (2025). NAB is adding 237 offshore roles while cutting domestic headcount. This creates a reputational risk purchase: Sentriq is proof that offshore hiring has integrity controls. The buyer is not CISO — it is the Board Risk Committee.

3. Israel is the home market with a war-footing pitch

In Israel, the pitch is not EU AI Act and not purely DPRK. It is hostile state penetration of defense hiring under wartime conditions. Iran, Hamas, and Hezbollah have documented operations targeting Israeli defense contractors via fake identities. Rafael hired 1,800 employees in 2024 under wartime production pressure — exactly the conditions where hiring integrity fails. Samuel and Ben have direct cultural and network access to every Israeli defense company. Fastest possible sales cycle globally.
