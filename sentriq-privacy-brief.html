0. The Problem Sentriq Solves (Read First)
Context for understanding why this product exists and what it does. Not a legal section.
The Market Problem

The hiring pipeline is under attack from multiple directions simultaneously. AI resume generators, mass-apply bots, and proxy fraud networks have made it impossible for employers to trust what arrives in their applicant queue. One operations role at a recent high-profile company drew 6,500 applications in two weeks. 41% of companies have already hired a fraudulent candidate, many of whom gained access to internal systems.

At the extreme end, North Korean state-backed operatives use stolen identities, fabricated GitHub histories, and live AI coaching during video interviews to gain employment at technology companies. One such operative passed four video interviews and a full background check before being discovered (KnowBe4, 2024). The US DOJ and FBI have issued coordinated indictments and public warnings.

No infrastructure layer exists to detect this at the point of application, before any recruiter time, interview slot, or system access is involved. Sentriq builds that layer.

The Candidate Hiring Captcha

Sentriq's product is called the Candidate Hiring Captcha. The name is intentional: just as a website CAPTCHA distinguishes a human from a bot before granting access, Sentriq's system surfaces a trust signal that distinguishes an authentic candidate from a fraudulent one, before the application enters the employer's ATS (Applicant Tracking System).

Sentriq does not block or reject candidates automatically. It surfaces a signal. Humans make the final decision. This architecture is intentional: it avoids automated decision-making liability and keeps a human in the loop at every stage.

Why Existing Tools Cannot Solve This

Background check companies (Checkr, HireRight): Run after 6+ interview rounds and $1,900+ in spent cost. Verify identity documents, not behavioral intent. DPRK operatives use real stolen SSNs and pass them.

ATS platforms (Greenhouse, Lever, Comeet): Score candidate fit. They do not detect fraud. Their customer contracts prohibit sharing candidate data between employers, making a cross-company fraud signal structurally impossible.

Identity verification platforms (Persona, Checkr IDV): Confirm a face matches a document. Cannot detect proxy fraud (right document, wrong person operating the account), behavioral anomalies, or coordinated campaigns.

Deepfake detection at interviews (Clarity, GetReal): Operate at the video interview stage, after more than $1,700 in process cost is already spent. Complement to Sentriq, not a substitute.

1. Product Overview
What Sentriq does and who the parties are in plain language.
What Sentriq Is

Sentriq is a pre-application integrity layer for hiring. It sits between the candidate and the employer's ATS (Applicant Tracking System). When a candidate fills out a job application form, Sentriq captures behavioral signals to determine whether the application is being made by an authentic human, a bot, a proxy, or an AI-assisted fraudulent actor.

Sentriq is not a product that makes hiring decisions. It surfaces a trust signal that humans review. No candidate is automatically rejected. All flags route to human review.

The Three Parties

Employer (Sentriq Customer)

  • Integrates Sentriq into their application form
  • Receives the trust signal output
  • Sees raw behavioral data only when a high-probability fraud event is escalated
  • CISO owns outcome on confirmed fraud cases
  • Signs contract with Sentriq

Candidate (Data Subject)

  • Applies for a job on the employer's platform
  • Unaware of Sentriq during passive layer
  • Redirected to Sentriq-hosted page if flagged for async challenge
  • Gives consent to Sentriq only at the async challenge stage
  • Has data rights (extent TBD by counsel)

Sentriq

  • Operates the passive behavioral capture embedded in employer's form
  • Hosts the async challenge experience
  • Employs analysts who review flagged cases
  • Operates the cross-company fraud graph
  • Neutral third party, not the employer, not the candidate's agent

Sentriq Analyst

  • Internal Sentriq staff member
  • Reviews flagged candidate signals
  • Accesses raw behavioral data only for flagged cases
  • Does not make the final hiring decision (employer does)

4. Layer 1: Passive Behavioral Capture
Embedded in the employer's application form. Candidate is unaware.

Sentriq's SDK is embedded inside the employer's existing job application form. As the candidate fills in the form fields, Sentriq captures behavioral metadata passively in the background. No separate screen, no redirect, no visible Sentriq branding. The candidate does not know Sentriq is present.

High Exposure: Processing Without Candidate Awareness

At Layer 1, Sentriq processes candidate personal data (IP, device fingerprint, behavioral signals) without the candidate's direct knowledge. The legal basis for this processing is not yet determined. Counsel must advise on whether legitimate interests, employer contract necessity, or explicit consent is required: and whether employer disclosure is sufficient.

Open: Behavioral Biometric Classification

Keystroke dynamics and mouse movement patterns may constitute biometric data under GDPR Article 9 (special category) or under US state laws (Illinois BIPA, Texas CUBI, Washington MY). Counsel to classify. If classified as biometric, a separate legal basis and likely explicit consent will be required.

5. Layer 2: Async Challenge (Sentriq-Hosted)
Triggered only when Layer 1 surfaces anomalies. Candidate is redirected to a Sentriq-hosted page and becomes aware of Sentriq for the first time.
How it works

When Layer 1 flags a candidate, they are redirected from the employer's application form to a Sentriq-hosted web page. This is analogous to the 3D Secure redirect that occurs when a bank wants to verify a card transaction during e-commerce checkout: the candidate leaves the employer's environment and enters Sentriq's. At this point, Sentriq presents its identity, explains the purpose, and obtains candidate consent before proceeding.

The candidate completes a short structured task designed to distinguish authentic human capability from AI-generated or proxy responses. Questions are designed around personal experience that AI cannot fabricate (e.g., "Describe the most interesting non-professional conversation you had with a manager. What was the topic?").

High Exposure: Voice Recording

The AI phone call option involves recording a candidate's voice. Voice biometrics are explicitly classified as special category data under GDPR Article 9. In the US, California (CCPA/CPRA), Illinois (BIPA), Texas, and Washington have specific biometric data laws. Some US states (California, Florida, others) require all-party consent for call recording. Counsel must advise before this feature is built.

Founder Decision: Consent at Redirect

Sentriq's current intent is to obtain candidate consent at the point of the async challenge redirect: before any Layer 2 data is collected. The consent mechanism, wording, and scope are open for counsel to design. The consent timing (redirect only, not at application start) is a founder position that counsel should validate.

6. Layer 3: Human Review & CISO Escalation
Sentriq analysts review the full signal stack. Employer CISO makes the final call.
How it works

Flagged candidates from Layer 2 are reviewed by a Sentriq analyst. The analyst has access to the full behavioral signal stack and the async challenge responses. The analyst does not make the hiring decision. They produce a structured assessment of the fraud signal that is shared with the employer. The employer's CISO or designated security officer determines whether the case is a confirmed fraud event.

Only CISO-confirmed fraud cases proceed to the cross-company graph. All other flagged cases that are not confirmed as fraud are retained by Sentriq per retention policy but do not enter the graph.

The HR-to-CISO Escalation Path

The bottom bar shows how the employer's data exposure expands at each stage. At Step 1, the employer sees only a trust signal. Raw behavioral data is not shared until the case becomes a security incident (Step 3). Only after CISO confirmation does data enter the cross-company graph.

Why the Escalation Trigger Matters for Regulation

The shift from HR case to CISO case is not just an internal workflow: it changes the data access, the legal basis for further processing, and potentially the controller/processor relationship. At the HR stage, the employer receives a signal and Sentriq retains the underlying data. At the CISO stage, raw personal data is shared with a new actor (the CISO) who becomes a recipient under GDPR. This escalation path needs a documented legal basis and should be reflected in the employer DPA. Counsel to advise on what triggers, documentation, and disclosures are required at each transition point.

Exposure: EU AI Act: Automated Profiling

Sentriq's scoring of candidates constitutes automated profiling that produces a significant effect on their employment prospects. Under GDPR Article 22 and the EU AI Act (hiring AI = high risk, Annex III), specific obligations apply: human oversight (present), right to explanation, bias testing, audit trails. Sentriq's human-in-the-loop architecture partially addresses this. Counsel to map full EU AI Act compliance pathway.

Open: Candidate Right to Explanation

Under GDPR Article 22 and the EU AI Act, candidates subject to automated decision-making have a right to an explanation and to contest the outcome. At what point and in what form does Sentriq or the employer provide this? What can be disclosed without compromising the detection methodology? Counsel to advise.

7. Cross-Company Fraud Graph
The network intelligence layer. Only confirmed fraud cases enter. Shared across all Sentriq customers.
Architecture principle

The cross-company graph requires Sentriq to operate as a neutral third party. It receives a CISO-confirmed fraud signal from Employer A and uses derived identifiers from that candidate (device fingerprint, behavioral hash, resume hash) to flag matching patterns when the same candidate applies to Employer B. Employers do not share data directly with each other. All sharing occurs through Sentriq's infrastructure.

High Exposure: Cross-Border Sharing Without Candidate Knowledge

Data about a candidate confirmed as fraudulent at Employer A (e.g., a US company) may be shared via the graph with Employer B (e.g., an EU company). The candidate had no relationship with Employer B at the time their data was entered into the graph. This raises questions about: lawful basis for cross-employer sharing, cross-border transfer mechanisms (GDPR Chapter V), and whether the candidate must be notified of inclusion in the fraud graph. Counsel to advise.

Founder Decision: Only CISO-Confirmed Fraud Enters the Graph

Founders have decided that the graph accepts only candidates explicitly confirmed as fraudulent by an employer CISO. Unconfirmed flags and cleared candidates do not enter the graph. This is a quality and liability design decision. Counsel should validate whether this threshold is sufficient from a data protection standpoint, and what documentation the CISO confirmation must include.

8. Consent Architecture
When the candidate is made aware of Sentriq and when consent is obtained.
Founder Position: Consent at Async Challenge Only

Current intent is to obtain consent at the point of async challenge redirect (Layer 2), not at the start of the application. The employer is responsible for any disclosure at Layer 1. Counsel must assess whether this is sufficient for GDPR, EU AI Act, and US state law compliance, and design the consent wording and scope.

Open: Does Layer 2 Consent Cover Graph Entry?

The consent obtained at Layer 2 covers the async challenge. Whether it also covers entry into the cross-company fraud graph (a separate, ongoing use of the data beyond the original employment context) is an open question. Counsel to determine whether a separate consent or legal basis is required for graph inclusion, and what the candidate must be told about it.

Open: Employer Disclosure Obligations at Layer 1

If the legal basis for Layer 1 processing rests on the employer's legitimate interests or their contract with the candidate (job application), the employer's privacy notice or application disclosure may need to reference Sentriq. What exactly must employers disclose? What template language must Sentriq contractually require of employers? Counsel to design.

11. Open Questions for Counsel
All items below are open. No position has been taken by founders on these.
  • Controller vs. Processor classification. At Layer 1, Sentriq processes candidate data embedded in the employer's form. At Layer 2, Sentriq operates its own hosted service and collects data directly. For the fraud graph, Sentriq operates as an independent data controller. What is the correct classification at each layer, and what contractual structure does this require (DPA, joint controller agreement)?
  • Legal basis for Layer 1 passive collection. What is the lawful basis under GDPR Article 6 for processing candidate data at the passive layer, where the candidate has no direct relationship with Sentriq and no consent has been obtained?
  • Behavioral biometric classification. Do keystroke dynamics, mouse movement patterns, and typing behavior constitute biometric data under GDPR Article 9, BIPA, or other relevant laws? If yes, what changes to consent architecture and legal basis are required?
  • Voice data classification and call recording compliance. The AI phone call option involves recording candidate voice. Is this biometric data? What consent is required for recording under applicable state and federal laws? What restrictions apply to the resulting transcript?
  • Adequacy of employer-side disclosure at Layer 1. What must employers include in their job application privacy notice or terms for Sentriq's Layer 1 processing to be lawful? What template language must Sentriq require of employers contractually?
  • Consent scope for graph entry. Does consent obtained at Layer 2 (async challenge) extend to inclusion in the cross-company fraud graph? Or does graph entry require a separate consent or a different legal basis entirely?
  • Cross-border data transfers. If a candidate applies from an EU country and their (hashed) data enters the fraud graph, and that graph serves US and non-EU employers, what transfer mechanism applies? SCCs? Adequacy decision?
  • ePrivacy / cookie consent for SDK. Sentriq's Layer 1 SDK performs browser fingerprinting inside the employer's web form. Does this constitute tracking subject to ePrivacy Directive consent requirements? Does responsibility fall on Sentriq or the employer?
  • EU AI Act compliance pathway. Sentriq's scoring of candidates in a hiring context is high-risk under Annex III. What is the full compliance pathway: conformity assessment, registration, human oversight documentation, bias audit, transparency notice to candidates?
  • EEOC / disparate impact exposure. What testing and documentation is required to demonstrate that Sentriq's behavioral signals do not produce disparate impact on protected classes? What ongoing audit obligation exists?
  • Data storage geography and infrastructure. Where should candidate data be stored (cloud region, jurisdiction) to minimize transfer risk and comply with data localization preferences? Founders are open to any recommendation.
  • Retention periods. What are the appropriate retention periods for: Layer 1 signals (non-flagged candidates), Layer 2 challenge responses, fraud graph entries, consent records, CISO confirmation records?
  • Data subject access requests. If a candidate submits a GDPR Article 15 request or a CCPA "right to know" request, what data must Sentriq disclose? Can Sentriq withhold behavioral signal detail to protect detection methodology?
  • Age verification. Job applicants could include individuals under 18 in some jurisdictions. Does Sentriq need an age-gating mechanism? What applies to the phone call feature specifically?
12. Founder Decisions Requiring Validation
These are positions founders have already taken. Counsel should validate, challenge, or redesign where needed.
  • Consent at Layer 2 only. The current plan is to obtain candidate consent only at the point of async challenge redirect. No consent or disclosure from Sentriq at Layer 1. Employer is responsible for Layer 1 disclosure. Founders request counsel validate whether this is legally sufficient or whether earlier Sentriq-side consent or disclosure is required.
  • Only CISO-confirmed fraud cases enter the graph. No unconfirmed flags, no borderline cases, no cleared candidates. Counsel should validate whether this threshold is sufficient and what documentation the CISO confirmation must contain to be defensible.
  • Fraudulent candidates forfeit deletion rights. Founders' intent is that candidates confirmed as fraudulent cannot force deletion of their graph entry. Counsel must advise on the legal mechanism that supports this (if any) and what documentation is required. Alternative approaches (legitimate interests, legal claims basis) should be assessed.
  • Extended retention of confirmed fraud data. Founders wish to retain confirmed fraud entries in the graph for as long as legally permissible, to protect employers from repeat fraud attempts. Counsel to determine maximum retention period and required justification.
  • Employer sees raw signals only on high-probability fraud events. In normal flow, employers receive only the trust signal output. Raw behavioral data is disclosed to the employer only when a case is escalated as a high-probability fraud event. Counsel to validate this is consistent with the DPA structure and that it is clearly documented in the employer contract.
  • Human-in-the-loop as core architecture. No automated rejections. All flags route to human review. Counsel should validate that this architecture satisfies GDPR Article 22 requirements and EU AI Act human oversight obligations, and whether any further documentation or process is required.
  • EU compliance from day one. Founders intend to accept EU employers and EU candidates from the first commercial deployment, not as a later phase. Counsel should scope the minimum viable compliance package required before the first EU customer is onboarded.
Note on Product Status

Sentriq has no live product in production as of April 2026. There is a working demo only. This briefing describes the intended architecture. All compliance work should be completed before production deployment. The founders have explicitly committed to following counsel's guidance on data architecture, storage geography, consent flows, and contractual structure.
